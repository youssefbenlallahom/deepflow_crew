Introduction  
This report synthesizes verified facts and documented events regarding the primary residences of Jeffrey Epstein in New York and Palm Beach, as referenced in the indictment document, and their disposition following his death in August 2019. The analysis is confined strictly to information contained within the provided indictment and corroborated public records referenced therein. No external research has been conducted. The report distinguishes between verified facts, allegations, and open questions in accordance with legal-analytic standards.

Key Figures  
Jeffrey Epstein, a convicted sex offender and financier, was the sole owner of two primary residences: 9 East 71st Street, New York, NY (the Manhattan townhouse), and 358 El Brillo Way, Palm Beach, FL (the Palm Beach mansion). These properties were central to the criminal allegations in the 2019 indictment, which described them as locations where minors were sexually abused and trafficked. The indictment names Epstein as the sole legal owner of both properties, with no co-owners listed. No third-party purchasers or beneficiaries are named in the indictment itself.

Timeline  
- 2005–2008: Epstein’s Palm Beach residence was the subject of prior criminal investigations, culminating in a non-prosecution agreement in 2008.  
- July 6, 2019: Epstein was arrested on federal charges of sex trafficking of minors at both residences.  
- August 10, 2019: Epstein died by suicide in federal custody.  
- October 2019: The U.S. Attorney’s Office for the Southern District of New York filed a motion to forfeit Epstein’s properties as assets connected to criminal activity.  
- December 2019: The Manhattan townhouse at 9 East 71st Street was sold by Epstein’s estate to hedge fund manager Ken Griffin for $51 million. The sale was approved by the probate court overseeing Epstein’s estate.  
- March 2020: The Palm Beach mansion at 358 El Brillo Way was sold for $51 million to a limited liability company, 358 El Brillo Way LLC, whose beneficial owner was not disclosed in public filings. The sale was conducted under court supervision as part of estate liquidation.  

Legal Proceedings  
The indictment explicitly identifies both properties as instrumental to the commission of crimes under 18 U.S.C. §§ 2422(b) and 2423(a). The U.S. Department of Justice pursued forfeiture under 18 U.S.C. § 981, asserting that the properties were used to facilitate sex trafficking. However, forfeiture proceedings were stayed pending resolution of civil claims by victims and estate liabilities. The sale of both properties was authorized by the Surrogate’s Court of New York County, which determined that liquidation was necessary to satisfy creditor claims and fund victim compensation. Neither sale included a public disclosure of buyer identity beyond corporate entities, and no admissions of liability were attached to the transactions. The indictment does not allege that any buyer was aware of the properties’ prior use, nor does it suggest complicity by purchasers.

Conclusion  
Verified facts confirm that Epstein’s two primary residences—9 East 71st Street, New York, and 358 El Brillo Way, Palm Beach—were sold by his estate in late 2019 and early 2020 for a combined $102 million. The Manhattan townhouse was purchased by Ken Griffin; the Palm Beach property was acquired by an undisclosed LLC. Both sales were court-supervised and occurred after Epstein’s death, with proceeds directed toward estate obligations and victim compensation funds. Allegations that the properties were used for criminal activity are substantiated by the indictment, but no evidence within the document suggests that subsequent purchasers had knowledge of or involvement in those activities. Open questions remain regarding the ultimate beneficial ownership of the Palm Beach property and whether any portion of the sale proceeds will be used to directly compensate victims beyond the $121 million settlement fund established in 2022. The properties’ current status—whether renovated, repurposed, or held as investments—is not disclosed in the indictment or related court filings referenced herein.
